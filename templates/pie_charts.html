Pie Charts

Our pie charts that display the distribution of age, sex, and race of the shooting victims.

Our findings: The age group most likely to be shot by police are people in the 25 - 34 years old range, while anyone in the younger than 18 age group is the least likely.

Our findings: Males are substantially more likely to be the victim of police shootings than women.

Our findings: White Americans make up just over half of the victims in our dataset. Taking into consideration the proportion of the population that is white, however, we found that white people (75% of the population) are under-represented while Black Americans (12% of the population) are over-represented.
